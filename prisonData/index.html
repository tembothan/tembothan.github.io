"Neither slavery nor involuntary servitude, except as a punishment for crime whereof the party shall have been duly convicted, shall exist within the United States, or any place subject to their jurisdiction."

It is because of this that business in the United States has made tremendous monetary profit. Many business today collaborate with prison to use prison labor as a means of production. This project explores and exposes the companies in the United States that use prison labor, how much they gain as a at the expense of inmate of it and how much inmates receive in monetary compensation.



How much do different sized companies make off prison labor?


Hundreds of thousands of businesses around the world user prison labor to help support their revenue. The graph above shows 1370 businesses and compares their annual revenue in the millions between 2004 and 2020. Companies like Amazon, AT&T, IBM, McKinsey & Company and many more make millions. An article from Yale's Institution for Social and Public Policy writes that "According to the Federal Bureau of Prisons, federal inmates earn 12 cents to 40 cents per hour for jobs serving the prison, and 23 cents to $1.15 per hour in Federal Prison Industries factories." Some states like Alabama pay inmates nothing for their labor. This money is often used by inmates in the commissary to buy things like:

  • Food (chips, soda, tea, soup, etc)
  • Clothing (shirts socks, thermal clothing, undergarments, etc)
  • Personal hygiene items (shaving cream, hair brush, deodorant, shampoo, dental supplies, etc)
  • Personal Items (pens, paper, books, stamps, religious items, etc)
